3D Design

3D design intro: 3D design is an intricate and extremely technical aspect of post-production in media. It requires great proficiency and a highly specialized skill set. Thankfully, these skills can be learned, and there is a wealth of information available on the subject. 3D design has multiple facets: The first idea that one must have about 3D design is that 3D design has many facets to it. It is not one practice or study, but multiple. The most fundamental of these and perhaps the first step in any 3D design process is the acquisition of or creation of a model. This is usually referred to simply as 3D modeling. 3D models: 3D modeling involves either purchasing a model from a website, a freelancer, a company, etc. In fact, even some movies or franchises will sell the rights to their models! Many more freelancers and individual content creators will create replicas of these models, and sometimes with extreme detail and effort, resulting in a product that may be even more hyper realistic than what was created for the original artwork. Creating your own models (doing your own modeling): It is also possible to create your own 3D models. Most models work around providing simple geometric shapes that can then be contorted, altered, and shaped to create models like people, animals, characters, etc. Most models you see in movies and television actually started in a simple program from shapes like cubes and spheres! 3D animation: The next component of 3D design, perhaps most commonly known, is animation. This involves using some 3D software (which we will get into later) to program the movement of your 3D model. This is critical in media types like video game design and modern cinema. Animation can be applied to biological models like humans and animals, it can also be applied to machines and other 3D created objects (a good example is the Transformers series), or it can even be applied to simple 3D text seen in a movie trailer. Animation is just as vital to the process as modeling. 3D design programs: With the fact that there are many different components of the 3D design subject, we can now explore the various programs and softwares available for different aspects of 3D design, what they do, some basic information about them, and what processes they specifically are used for.
